## 📋 What is LoopSnap?

LoopSnap is a lightweight screen recorder for macOS. It helps you capture parts of your screen and save them as GIFs. The app is simple and fast. It is built in Swift using native macOS tools. If you liked ScreenToGif on Windows, LoopSnap offers a similar experience for Mac users.

---

## 🖥️ System Requirements

Before you install LoopSnap, make sure your Mac meets these requirements:

- Running macOS 11.0 (Big Sur) or later
- At least 4 GB of RAM
- About 50 MB of free disk space
- A Retina or standard Mac display

The app uses the latest screen capture framework on macOS to give smooth results. It works best on newer Mac models.

## 🎥 How to Capture a GIF

LoopSnap focuses on capturing quick GIFs of your screen. Here's how to use it:

1. Open LoopSnap.
2. Click the **Record** button.
3. Click and drag to select the part of your screen you want to capture.
4. The recording starts immediately.
5. When done, click **Stop**.
6. Preview your GIF in the app.

---

## 💾 Exporting Your GIF

After recording, you can save your GIF file:

- Click **Export** in the app.
- Choose the folder where you want to save the GIF.
- Enter a name for your file.
- Click **Save**.

Your GIF is ready to use on websites, emails, or chats.

## ⚙️ Settings You Can Adjust

LoopSnap lets you control a few key options:

- **Frame rate**: Set how many frames per second the GIF captures. Higher means smoother but larger files.
- **GIF size**: Resize the GIF during export.
- **Loop count**: Choose if your GIF plays once, loops a few times, or loops endlessly.
- **Recording audio**: (Currently disabled, planned for future versions.)

## 🛠 Troubleshooting

### LoopSnap won’t open

- Check that your Mac meets system requirements.
- Make sure you installed LoopSnap correctly into the Applications folder.
- Try restarting your Mac and opening LoopSnap again.

### Screen capture doesn’t start

- Confirm LoopSnap has permission in **System Preferences** under **Screen Recording**.
- Restart LoopSnap after changing permissions.

### GIF is very large or choppy

- Lower the frame rate in settings.
- Reduce the size of the recording area.
